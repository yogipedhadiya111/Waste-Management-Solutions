The Waste Management Crisis in India

Understanding the scale and urgency of the problem

In FY 2021–22, India generated approximately 1.7 lakh tonnes of municipal solid waste daily, of which only 54% was scientifically treated. Around 24% was dumped in landfills and 22% remained unaccounted due to leakages in the waste management chain.

This unaccounted waste is often burned or disposed of in open areas, drains, or water bodies, posing severe environmental and public health risks. The situation calls for strict solutions to enable all waste generators to contribute to effective waste management.

Mandatory Training for Every Citizen

Similar to countries where military training is mandatory for every citizen, in India, there shall be mandatory Waste Management training for every Waste Generator/Citizen. This will ensure source segregation, resulting in better waste management and lesser load on facilities and landfills.

Types of Waste Management Facilities

Waste-to-Energy (WtE) Plants

Facilities that convert non-recyclable waste into electricity, heat, or fuel through various processes including incineration, gasification, pyrolysis, and anaerobic digestion.

Benefits:
  • Reduces waste volume by up to 90%
  • Generates renewable energy
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions
  • Decreases dependence on landfills
Biomethanization Plants

Facilities that use anaerobic digestion to break down organic waste and produce biogas, which can be used for cooking, electricity generation, or as vehicle fuel.

Benefits:
  • Produces renewable energy
  • Creates nutrient-rich digestate for fertilizer
  • Reduces methane emissions from organic waste
  • Provides clean cooking fuel
Material Recovery Facilities (MRFs)

Specialized plants that receive, separate, and prepare recyclable materials for marketing to end-user manufacturers.

Benefits:
  • Maximizes recycling rates
  • Creates jobs in the recycling industry
  • Reduces raw material extraction
  • Conserves natural resources
Composting Facilities

Facilities that process organic waste into compost through aerobic decomposition, creating a nutrient-rich soil amendment.

Benefits:
  • Reduces organic waste in landfills
  • Produces valuable soil conditioner
  • Improves soil health and fertility
  • Reduces need for chemical fertilizers
